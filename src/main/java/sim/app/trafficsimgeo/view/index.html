Agent-based simulation tool for the evaluation of traffic light configurations in road networks

By Dayan Bravo

By Mailyn Moreno

A poor traffic planning can bring with it countless negative effects such as collapse, traffic jams and accidents. To avoid this situation it is useful, before applying transitÂ´s configurations, to know the impact they will bring. In this way you can assess, with more confidence, if the configuration will be functional or not. Currently, process simulation is a solution to obtain such information. In dependency of the type of simulation, the results will be more or less close to reality. The agents-based simulation provides benefit in terms of simulations of road networks.

This paper describes the process of developing an agent-based simulation tool that contributes to the process of configuring traffic controllers. The development of this tool is based on previous technologies and experiences in order to achieve a result adjusted to the real needs and capabilities. The result is a GIS tool for agent-based simulation using the MASON framework.

With the development of this tool, analysts and traffic engineers have a tool that provides them with information that helps them analyze and make decisions regarding the problems related to the configuration of traffic controllers.

Keywords: Agent-based simulation, GIS, Intelligent agents, Semaphore, Traffic light.
